About This Project

Welcome to the Brown University Plant Mapping project! This initiative aims to collect and map various plant species across campus for BIOL 0430, The Evolution of Plant Diversity. By contributing your observations, you help create a comprehensive database of the plant species found here, making it easier for everyone to learn about the local flora. This project was developed by Brooke Wangenheim in Fall 2024 as part of Brown University’s Data Science Fellows program.

Notice Something Wrong?

Help us maintain the database by flagging incorrect information. Submit the upload ID (the number in parentheses next to the species) and a brief explanation of what is incorrect. For example, “genus is wrong” “plant doesn’t exist” or “typo in species”

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is this project about?
This project helps to map and document plant species found at Brown University.
How can I contribute to the map?
You can contribute by uploading observations of plants you find on campus through our upload page.
How do I flag incorrect information?
If you notice incorrect data in the database, you can use the form above to submit a flag and describe the issue.
Is there a way to get in touch with the project team?
Yes! Please reach out to Professor Rebecca Kartizinel.
